    Grid stress index

    Transparent 0-100 heuristic: loaded pressure factors are weighted, renormalised, and summed. Not an official security-of-supply measure.

    Stress index recipe

    The score is a weighted 0-100 blend of loaded live inputs only: demand vs recent p90 (20%), residual demand after wind/solar (17%), carbon intensity (15%), low wind vs capacity (15%), low solar vs capacity (9%), price vs recent p90 (9%), largest 3-hour wind/solar ramp (8%), and frequency deviation (7%). Missing inputs are skipped and the remaining weights are renormalised.

    How this score combines Each row below becomes a 0-100 pressure score; the cockpit multiplies it by the active weight and sums the contributions.

      Open-Meteo GFS/UKMO/ECMWF variables for the selected region/postcode: wind power proxy, solar radiation proxy, and synoptic risk drivers.

      Weather proxy recipe

      Each weather call asks Open-Meteo for GFS seamless, UKMO seamless and ECMWF IFS hourly fields. Wind weather uses model-averaged 100 m wind speed passed through a simple turbine power curve: 0 below 3 m/s, 100% from 12-25 m/s, and cut-out above 25 m/s. Solar weather uses shortwave radiation scaled against 750 W/m2 with a cloud-cover penalty. The percentages are weather potential indices, not grid generation shares; the adjacent ramp chart uses grid/Elexon/NESO GW forecasts where available.

          Source links, model assumptions and deployment notes

          This is a client-side cockpit using public APIs directly. Wind and solar capacity inputs are GW fallback values used only when live NESO/Elexon capacity data cannot be read; they convert weather indices into approximate MW. The stress index is explanatory: it is not an official grid adequacy, security-of-supply or balancing-cost metric.
